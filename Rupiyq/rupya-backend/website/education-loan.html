Common questions

Got Questions?

What courses are eligible for education loans?+
Undergraduate, postgraduate, professional degrees (MBA, CA, engineering, medicine, law) at recognised universities in India and abroad. Skill-based and vocational courses may qualify with select lenders.
Do I need collateral for an education loan?+
Loans up to ₹7.5 lakh typically don't require collateral but need a co-applicant (parent or guardian). Loans above ₹7.5 lakh may require property or financial securities as collateral.
When do I start repaying?+
Repayment begins 6–12 months after completing the course (moratorium period), giving you time to find employment. During the course, only simple interest is charged.
Can I get an education loan if I haven't received my admission letter yet?+
Most lenders require a confirmed admission letter. However, some lenders offer provisional approval for top-ranked institutions based on entrance exam results or shortlisting.
Is there a tax benefit on education loan interest?+
Yes. Under Section 80E of the Income Tax Act, the entire interest amount paid on education loans is deductible — with no upper limit — for a maximum of 8 years.
